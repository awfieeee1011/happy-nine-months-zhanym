From a nervous "let's go" at Irufushi to this exact day.

August 2025 — Irufushi

"Let's go."

The first words I ever said to you at work. I was so nervous I couldn't think of anything better — and somehow that was enough to start everything.

October 18, 2025

Five days in Male

You'd already resigned. Before your flight back to Almaty, we had five days in Male City — and I finally asked you to be mine. You flew out on the 21st, and nothing about missing you has felt ordinary since.

December 11, 2025

Ten days, Male again

You came back. Ten days that reminded me the distance was worth it every single time.

February 12, 2026 — Hanoi

A month in Vietnam

After a few fights that taught us how to fight better, we gave ourselves a whole month — cuddling, wandering, trying everything once. The longest stretch we'd ever had together, and still not long enough.

June 12–18, 2026

Male, before Antalya

The longest we'd ever been apart came right before this — and then five more days together, before you flew off to start your job in Antalya.

Today — July 18, 2026

Nine months in

Three countries, a dozen goodbyes at airports, and you've built me from a boy into a man. I don't think there's a better woman out there for me.

A Letter

For you

Zhanym, nine months ago I said "let's go" to you because it was the only thing my nervous brain could find. I didn't know it would end up being the truest thing I've ever said — because I'd go anywhere, every time, if it means getting to you.

We've done this across Male and Almaty, across a month in Hanoi, and now across whatever distance sits between here and Antalya. The goodbyes at the airport never get easier, but they've never once made me want this less.

You took a boy who could barely get a sentence out the first time he saw you, and you built him into a man who knows exactly what he wants. That's you. Here's to the ninth month, and every one after it.
